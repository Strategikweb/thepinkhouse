About the House

In the area

2 km
Poça

Nearest beach

Enclosed between two coastal cliffs, Poça Beach is the nearest beach to the Studio. Poça Beach has good points of access from the Marginal coast road and a pedestrian tunnel that goes under the train tracks. Offers a promenade, life-guard, showers, toilets, bars and restaurants with ocean view.

EEC Blue Flag

2.7 km
Tamariz

Central location

Tamariz Beach is one of the most popular beaches given its central location and the close proximity of the railway station. Tamariz Beach has a complete range of services and facilities including an Atlantic saltwater swimming pool, promenade, showers, toilets, bars and restaurants with ocean view.

EEC Blue Flag

9 km
Carcavelos

Sport paradise

Carcavelos Beach is the largest expanse of sand on the Estoril coast and regularly hosts beach volleyball and football competitions. Given its easy access, Carcavelos Beach is highly popular during the summer while in winter it is mostly used for surfing and bodyboarding. Carcavelos offers a wide choice of restaurants and bars.

EEC Blue Flag

12 km
Guincho

Wind and waves

Part of the Sintra-Cascais Natural Park, Guincho Beach is endowed with a surrounding landscape of great beauty. Given both its strong winds and waves, Guincho attracts bodyboarders and surfers. Nearby, there are some excellent restaurants specialising in fish and shellfish dishes.

EEC Blue Flag

Things to do

Cascais

Cascais is a very lively and cosmopolitan town. Particularly recommended is a stroll through its streets, where you'll find nice shops, or enjoy the many outdoor cafés and restaurants scattered around town. Other than the beautiful beaches, visit the Forte, Marina and the Museums in the centre of the town.

Lisbon

Standing on the estuary of the River Tejo, the city has a long history dating back to the Romans. Lisbon offers a combination of old-world history, a great climate and safety. I strongly suggest you take a tuk tuk tour to help you manage Lisbon's seven hills and its paved steep streets. Must see places: Castelo São Jorge, Alfama, Mosteiro dos Jerónimos, Torre de Belém, Terreiro do Paço and Cais das Colunas, Chiado and Bairro Alto.

Sintra

Hidden among a majestic forest, whimsical palaces, extravagant villas and the ruins of a 10th-century Moorish castle, Sintra is a swirl of fairy-tale styles, combining exotic and local architecture with beautiful views. The summer destination of choice for the nobility and bourgeoisie since the 15th century, Sintra's hills are filled with remarkable monuments and parks, including the National Palace of Sintra.
